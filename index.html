Bio

Xin Wang received the B.Sc. degree in electrical and communications engineering from Taiyuan University of Technology, Taiyuan, China, in 2006, and the M.Sc. and Ph.D. degrees in electrical and computer engineering from Marquette University, Milwaukee, WI, USA in 2008 and 2011, respectively. Between 2011 and 2013, he served as an assistant professor at the Oregon Institute of Technology, and a research faculty member at the Oregon Renewable Energy Center and the Oregon BEST. In 2013, he joined the faculty of the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the Southern Illinois University, Edwardsville, IL, USA, where he is currently an associate professor. He is also the director of power systems control group at Southern Illinois University Edwardsville, focusing on designing power electronic applications and developing automatic control strategies for power and energy systems.


His current research interests include power systems, power electronics, electric machines, and control problems related to smart grid, robotics, and electrified vehicles. He has authored or co-authored more than 60 archival journal and conference publications in related areas.


He has been serving on the editorial boards of many journals and conferences in power systems, industrial electronics, and control areas. Prof. Xin Wang is a senior member of the IEEE, ASME.
